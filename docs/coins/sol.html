Analysis

Highest Alpha, Worst Risk Metrics

SOL shows a paradox: highest alpha (+26.04%) but worst risk-adjusted returns. This is because the market crashed -30.08%, so even a -4.04% loss represents significant outperformance.

Why SOL Struggles

  • Extreme volatility - SOL had the largest price swings of all assets
  • Lower liquidity - Smaller OI pools create noisier signals
  • Bucket sizing issues - $0.50 buckets may be inappropriate for volatile asset
  • Higher class imbalance - scale_pos_weight=1.95 indicates severe imbalance

Risk Concerns

  • Win rate 42.7% - Below breakeven, needs strong R:R to compensate
  • Profit factor 0.82 - Losses larger than wins on average
  • Sharpe -3.78 - Extremely poor risk-adjusted returns
  • Max drawdown 5.11% - Highest of all assets tested

Recommendations

Do Not Trade

SOL strategy is not suitable for production:

  • Profit factor < 1 means guaranteed losses over time
  • Win rate below 50% with current R:R
  • Alpha is a result of market crash, not strategy edge

Potential Fixes

  • Significantly higher confidence threshold (reduce trades)
  • Wider stop loss (2-3%) to avoid whipsaw
  • Consider SOL-specific features (validator metrics, ecosystem TVL)
  • Use as contrarian signal (fade the predictions)
  • Or simply avoid trading SOL with this strategy
