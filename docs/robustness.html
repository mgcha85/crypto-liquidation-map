Multi-Timeframe Robustness Analysis

Extended validation across 3 coins, 3 timeframes, and 12-month out-of-sample test period.

Key Finding: Strategy Shows Regime Dependence

When extending the test period from 4 months to 12 months, all configurations show negative Sharpe ratios. The strategy works in specific market regimes (Q1 2026) but does not generalize to all conditions.

Why SOTA Works but Extended Fails

SOTA Success Factors

  • Regime fit: Q1 2026 had specific liquidation patterns
  • Selective trading: 6h sampling = fewer, better signals
  • Short test: 4 months of favorable conditions
  • Low trade count: 24 trades minimizes noise

Extended Failure Factors

  • Regime mismatch: Strategy doesn't adapt to all conditions
  • Overtrading: 1,000+ trades includes weak signals
  • Long test: 12 months spans multiple regimes
  • No regime filter: Model trades in unfavorable conditions

Recommendations

For Live Trading

  1. Add regime filter: Only trade when market conditions match training regime
  2. Reduce trade frequency: Use 6h+ sampling or confidence threshold >0.7
  3. Start with SOL 1h: Best performer in extended test (Sharpe +0.11)
  4. No leverage: Until strategy shows consistent positive returns
  5. Paper trade first: Validate in real-time before capital deployment

For Research

  1. Develop regime detection model (bull/bear/sideways classifier)
  2. Train separate models per regime
  3. Add macro features (funding rate, exchange flows, fear/greed index)
  4. Explore ensemble of timeframes with voting
  5. Test walk-forward optimization with monthly retraining

Methodology

Optimization Setup

  • Optimizer: Optuna TPE sampler, 50 trials per configuration
  • Objective: Maximize Sharpe ratio on test set
  • Model: XGBoost classifier with scale_pos_weight for class imbalance
  • Features: Full liquidation map features (20+) + OHLCV candle features
  • Labels: Triple barrier (PT=2%, SL=1%, horizon=24-48h depending on timeframe)
  • Train/Test: Time-based split (last 12 months = test)
